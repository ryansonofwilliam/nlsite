Loft Insultation
•
Loft insulation has been shown to be the most cost-
effective insulation method.
•
Can be applied to either the rafters or the joists.
•
Costs are around £10-£20/m² for joists and £20-£40 /m²
for rafters.
•
Already present in the dwellings at New Lanark so not
applicable to this project.

Internal Wall Insulation
•
Has no visual impact on the building externally.
•
Costs are higher as the building walls are solid.
•
Costs are estimated to be between £55-£140/m² for
Internal solid wall insulation.
•
Care must be taken to leave a sufficient air gap to
minimise damp forming.
•
There is currently no internal wall insulation in the
dwellings at New Lanark.

Windows
•
Windows typically account for 10% of heat losses
within a home.
•
Options include double, triple and secondary
glazing.
•
Costs from £100 to over £500 per window
depending on technology.
•
It is not possible to replace any windows at New
Lanark due to building restrictions.

External Wall Insulation
•
Insulation is applied to the outside of the building.
•
Reduces damping problems associated with internal
wall insulation.
•
Protects the outer wall from weathering.
•
Less disruptive and cheaper than internal wall
insulation.
•
Not possible at New Lanark due to building
restrictions.

Airtightness

• EnerPHIT and Passivhaus both require strict air tightening
– normally using air-tightness membranes.

• In historic buildings, odd geometry can lead to difficulties
and higher costs.

bridging.
